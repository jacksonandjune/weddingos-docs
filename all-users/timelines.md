# Timelines

The Timeline tab on a client's detail page is where you build and manage the day-of timeline for their wedding. Everything from getting ready through the reception lives here — organized by category, shareable with the wedding party, and exportable as a PDF.

---

## Getting there

Open a client's detail page and click the **Timeline** tab.

---

## Creating a timeline

If no timeline exists yet, you'll see a prompt to create one. Choose a scenario template that matches the wedding setup:

| Scenario | Use when |
|---|---|
| First Look — One Venue | Couple does a first look, ceremony and reception at same location |
| No First Look — One Venue | No first look, ceremony and reception at same location |
| First Look — Two Venues | Couple does a first look, different ceremony and reception venues |
| No First Look — Two Venues | No first look, different ceremony and reception venues |

Your agency's scenario templates define the default items and timing for each scenario. Select the one that fits and WeddingOS generates the timeline automatically — you edit from there.

---

## How timing works

All timeline items are anchored to the **ceremony start time**. Each item is stored as an offset in minutes from that anchor (negative = before ceremony, positive = after).

When you set the ceremony time on the timeline, all item times calculate automatically. Change the ceremony time and everything shifts with it — you don't have to manually update every item.

---

## Editing the timeline

Once created, you can:
- **Add items** — name, category, time, and whether it's internal or shared with the wedding party
- **Edit items** inline — click any item to update it
- **Reorder items** — drag to rearrange within a category
- **Delete items** — remove anything that doesn't apply

Items are organized by category (getting ready, portraits, ceremony, cocktail hour, reception, etc.). Your agency controls the category list under **Settings → Timelines**.

---

## Internal vs. shared items

Each timeline item has a visibility setting:

- **Internal** — visible to your team only, not shown on the wedding party share link
- **Shared** — visible to everyone, including the wedding party link

Vendor logistics, behind-the-scenes notes, and anything the couple doesn't need to see should stay internal. The ceremony order, portrait schedule, and reception flow are typically shared.

---

## Sharing with the wedding party

Once the timeline is ready, click **Share** to generate a shareable link. The link opens a clean, branded, read-only view showing only the shared items — no login required.

You can send this link to the couple, wedding party, or vendors. It stays live until you change it. If you update shared items after sending the link, the page reflects the changes automatically.

---

## Exporting to PDF

Click **Export PDF** to download a branded PDF of the timeline. The PDF includes all items (internal and shared) and is formatted for printing or attaching to a vendor briefing email.

---

## Scenario templates

Your admin manages the default scenario templates under **Settings → Timelines**. Templates define the starting items and times for each scenario type. Changes to templates don't affect timelines already created — they only apply when creating a new timeline.
